GutChecks: Red Flags & Safety 
Complete Site-Wide Recommendations v3 
Full audit: Homepage, Learn More, About, Resources, Contact, Footer | April 2026 
This document is a complete top-to-bottom audit of mygutcheck.org, covering every tab on the site. It addresses: (1) sections previously recommended but not yet implemented; (2) typography issues where titles are smaller than their subheadings; (3) removal of all em dashes in favour of semicolons or commas; (4) testimonial signature formatting; (5) the CTA update from "Try it free" to "Experience the app for free"; and (6) full rewrites of About, Resources, and Contact tabs. 
On the CTA change: Yes, update to "Experience the app for free" 
"Try it free" is functional but conventional; it reads as a product trial. "Experience the app for free" reframes the action from a transactional trial into an invitation. It suggests value and immersion rather than testing. It is also consistent with the language you are already using across your social content and scripts, which creates brand coherence when users move from TikTok or Instagram to the website. Update everywhere. 
On typography: titles smaller than subheadings 
This is a recurring issue across multiple sections. It happens when section eyebrows (e.g. "Why GutChecks Works Differently") are styled at a larger or heavier weight than the actual section title (e.g. "Built for red flags; everywhere"). Correct visual hierarchy should be: Section eyebrow (small, uppercase) > Section Title (largest) > Subtitle (medium) > Body (smallest). Full typography fix specifications are in Section 8 of this document.

15. Typography & Visual Hierarchy Fixes 
You flagged that titles are appearing smaller than subheadings in several places. This is a CSS hierarchy problem. Here are the exact sections affected and the fix specifications. 
Correct visual hierarchy (top to bottom) 
1. Section eyebrow (smallest, uppercase, muted colour): e.g. "WHY GUTCHECKS WORKS DIFFERENTLY" Suggested: 12-14px, uppercase, letter-spacing 1-2px, colour #64748b or brand accent 
2. Section title (largest, bold, primary colour): e.g. "Built for red flags; everywhere" 
Suggested: 32-40px desktop / 24-28px mobile, bold, colour #0f3460 or near-black 
3. Section subtitle (medium, regular): e.g. the intro sentence under the title 
Suggested: 16-18px, regular, colour #475569 
4. Card titles (smaller but still prominent): e.g. "Built for red flags; everywhere" (card version) Suggested: 18-20px, bold, colour #0f3460 
5. Card body (smallest body text): 
Suggested: 14-16px, regular, colour #475569 
Sections where titles appear smaller than eyebrows (needs fix) 
• Homepage — "Why GutChecks Works Differently" section (eyebrow vs title) 
• Homepage — "How It Works" section (eyebrow vs title) 
• Homepage — "Transparent Pricing" section (eyebrow vs "Simple pricing. Serious clarity.") • Homepage — "What Users Say" section (eyebrow vs "Why people use GutChecks") 
• Homepage — "Get the App" section (eyebrow vs "Available on All Devices") 
Additional typography notes 
• Ensure mobile responsiveness: text should scale down proportionally on smaller screens while maintaining hierarchy. • Titles should never be smaller than body text on any viewport. 
• Line height should be approximately 1.2 for titles, 1.5 for body text. 
• Maximum line length for body text: 65-75 characters; prevents walls of text and improves readability. • Use font-weight 700 or higher for titles; 400 for body; 500-600 for subtitles.
